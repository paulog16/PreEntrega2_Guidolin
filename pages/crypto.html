what are cryptocurrencies??

Cryptocurrencies are digital currencies that use cryptography to secure transactions and control the creation of new units. They are based on decentralized technologies, such as the chain of blocks (blockchain), which allows transactions to be recorded in a secure and transparent manner.

How are they different in real currencies?

Unlike traditional government-issued currencies, cryptocurrencies are decentralized and not controlled by any central authority, such as a central bank. This means that there is no intermediary, such as a bank, to validate the transactions. Instead, transactions are verified using cryptographic algorithms and recorded on a distributed network of computers.

It is the oldest and most well known cryptocurrency, with a large market capitalization and widespread adoption.

ETHEREUM

In addition to being a cryptocurrency, Ethereum is also a platform that enables the development of smart contracts and decentralized applications.
